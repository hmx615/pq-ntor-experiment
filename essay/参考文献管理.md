## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Post-Quantum Cryptography for Tor

[Berger2025] recently investigated PQC migration for Tor using a 9-node
Raspberry Pi testbed. They benchmarked ML-KEM, ML-DSA, and SLH-DSA,
reporting theoretical handshake latency of 161 Î¼s for ML-KEM-512.
However, their work:
(1) relied on theoretical estimation rather than full protocol implementation,
(2) tested only a single network topology, and
(3) did not consider satellite or SAGIN scenarios.

Our work addresses these limitations by providing a complete PQ-NTOR
implementation with end-to-end measurements, multi-topology validation,
and SAGIN network integration.

[Other Tor security works...]

### 2.2 NIST Post-Quantum Standardization

NIST standardized ML-KEM (formerly Kyber) [NIST2024] as the primary
KEM for post-quantum security. We adopt ML-KEM-512 (NIST Level 1) as
it provides adequate security equivalent to AES-128 while minimizing
bandwidth overheadâ€”critical for Tor's relay bandwidth constraints.

### 2.3 Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks

SAGIN architectures [Liu2018] combine satellite, aerial, and terrestrial
networks for global coverage. While prior work explored SAGIN routing
and handover [citations], none has addressed post-quantum security for
anonymous communication in SAGIN. Our work pioneers PQ-Tor deployment
in such environments.

### 2.4 Satellite Network Characteristics

Satellite networks exhibit high latency due to propagation delay: LEO
(5-10 ms), MEO (50-100 ms), GEO (250-280 ms) [DeCola2019]. We leverage
Skyfield [Rhodes2024] for accurate orbit modeling and visibility window
calculation, integrating real satellite dynamics into our experiments.
