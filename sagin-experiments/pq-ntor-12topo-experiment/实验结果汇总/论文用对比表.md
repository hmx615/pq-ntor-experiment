# 论文用对比表

## Table 1: PQ-NTOR Full Handshake性能对比

| Implementation | Platform | Architecture | Method | Full Handshake (μs) | vs Baseline |
|---------------|----------|--------------|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| Berger et al. [1] | Raspberry Pi 5 | ARM Cortex-A76 @ 2.4GHz | Theoretical estimation | 161.38 | Baseline |
| **Our Work** | **WSL2** | **x86_64** | **End-to-end measurement** | **31.00** | **5.2× faster** |
| **Our Work** | **Phytium Pi** | **ARM64 aarch64** | **End-to-end measurement** | **179.58** | **1.12× slower** |

**Notes:**
- Berger et al. used theoretical estimation by summing individual Kyber operations
- Our implementation measures complete PQ-NTOR handshake including HKDF and HMAC
- All tests used Kyber-512 from liboqs 0.11.0
- Our tests included 1000 iterations with 10 warmup rounds

---

## Table 2: PQ-NTOR操作分解 (μs)

| Operation | WSL2 (x86_64) | Phytium Pi (ARM64) | Ratio (ARM/x86) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Client create onionskin | 5.53 | 52.35 | 9.5× |
| Server create reply | 13.72 | 70.39 | 5.1× |
| Client finish handshake | 12.28 | 63.00 | 5.1× |
| **Full Handshake** | **31.00** | **179.58** | **5.8×** |

**Observation:** ARM64 platform shows 5.8× slowdown compared to x86_64, which is expected due to:
1. Lower CPU frequency
2. Different microarchitecture
3. Memory bandwidth differences

---

## Table 3: 性能稳定性分析

| Platform | Full Handshake Avg (μs) | Std Dev (μs) | Coefficient of Variation |
|----------|------------------------|--------------|------------------------|
| WSL2 (x86_64) | 31.00 | 3.90 | 12.6% |
| Phytium Pi (ARM64) | 179.58 | 2.02 | 1.1% |

**Analysis:** Both platforms show excellent stability with low standard deviation, indicating robust implementation quality.
